## Journal Scope

Bioinformatics (Oxford Academic) publishes research in the following areas:

- Computational biology algorithms and methods
- Sequence analysis and genome informatics
- Bioinformatics databases and data integration
- Systems biology modeling and simulation
- Structural bioinformatics
- Machine learning applications in biology
- Network analysis and pathway inference
- Text mining and literature analysis


## Manuscript Structure

### Required Section Order:

1. **Abstract**
2. **Introduction**
3. **Methods**
4. **Results**
5. **Discussion**
6. **Availability**
7. **Supplementary Materials**

### Required Sections:

- Abstract
- Introduction
- Methods
- Results
- Discussion
- Availability

### Optional Sections:

- Supplementary Materials
- Acknowledgements
- Funding

## Word Limits

**Total manuscript**: 6000 words

### Section-Specific Limits:

- **Abstract**: 150-250 words
- **Introduction**: 400-800 words
- **Methods**: 800-1600 words
- **Results**: 600-1200 words
- **Discussion**: 400-1000 words
- **Availability**: 50-150 words

## Formatting Requirements

- **Citation style**: numbered
- **Reference limit**: 50 references maximum
- **Figure limit**: 10 figures maximum
- **Table limit**: 5 tables maximum
- **Line spacing**: double
- **Font**: Times New Roman or Arial, 12pt

## Special Requirements

- Must include Data and Code Availability statement
- Abstract should be structured with Background/Results/Conclusions subsections
- Software tools must cite original publications
- Figures must be referenced in numerical order (Figure 1 before Figure 2)
- Methods section can be titled 'Methods', 'Algorithm', or 'Implementation'
- Results section can be titled 'Results' or 'Implementation'
- Availability section is mandatory for software/database papers
- Supplementary materials should include detailed algorithms, parameters, and additional results

## Section-Specific Citation Guidelines

**Introduction**: Cite relevant background literature and existing computational methods

**Methods**: Cite specific tools, algorithms, datasets, and software packages used

**Results**: Cite papers analyzed, benchmarked, or compared against

**Discussion**: Cite supporting or contrasting findings from the literature

**Availability**: Only cite platforms (Zenodo, GitHub, Bioconductor), not FAIR principles or general best practices
